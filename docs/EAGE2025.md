# EAGE 2025 study note

## Source publication

**Post-Stimulation Production Segmentation Prediction from Well-log Data: Leveraging Pipelines and SHAP for Interpretable Machine Learning Framework.**

Presented at the 86th EAGE Annual Conference & Exhibition, 2025, paper number 630.

## Research alignment

This repository reorganizes the accompanying training Notebook into an output-free, portable release. The implementation follows the published workflow:

- Formation-coefficient production segmentation using permeability, oil saturation, and porosity.
- A 2,800-sample, 33-well dataset with an 80/20 train-test split.
- StandardScaler or RobustScaler combined with PCA or PolynomialFeatures.
- XGBoost, Random Forest, and MLP regression models optimized by five-fold GridSearchCV.
- Taylor-diagram comparison and SHAP interpretation of the best-performing pipeline.

## Reported performance

The abstract reports PFS-NN as the highest-scoring pipeline with R2 = 0.96, PCC = 0.979, RMSE = 39.57 m3/d, MAE = 9.03 m3/d, and MSE = 1565.52.

## Interpretation

The published SHAP analysis identifies saturation-porosity interactions as influential second-order features. Gamma-ray-related terms show inhibitory effects at high values, while porosity combined with acoustic transit time has a strong positive influence on predicted production.
